Liboke de Poisson

Description

Liboke de poisson is a traditional Congolese dish made with fish wrapped in banana leaves and cooked with a flavorful sauce. The dish is known for its rich taste and is often served with rice or plantains.

Ingredients

  • Fresh fish (tilapia or catfish)
  • Banana leaves
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green bell pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Chili pepper (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or cilantro)

Steps

  1. Clean the fish and season with salt, black pepper, and chili pepper if desired.
  2. Chop the onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and green bell pepper.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and sauté the onions until translucent.
  4. Add the tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and green bell pepper to the pan and cook until the mixture is fragrant and well combined.
  5. Place a piece of banana leaf on a flat surface, add a portion of the sautéed mixture, and then place the seasoned fish on top.
  6. Wrap the banana leaf around the fish, securing it tightly.
  7. Repeat the process for all fish portions.
  8. Place the wrapped fish in a steamer or on a grill, and cook for about 30-40 minutes until the fish is cooked through and infused with the flavors of the sauce.
  9. Once cooked, carefully unwrap the banana leaves and serve the fish with the remaining sauce.
  10. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve hot with rice or plantains.

Notes

Liboke de poisson is a versatile dish that can be adapted with different types of fish or additional vegetables based on personal preference. The banana leaves not only impart a unique flavor but also keep the fish moist during cooking. This dish is often enjoyed during special occasions and gatherings in Congolese cuisine.
